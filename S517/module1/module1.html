The Folly of Prometheus

Sampo Jaakkola

As man craved for fire
So thought Prometheus
Their need seems dire
For he new well of mans lack
As he hated the nights without
a fire to warm ones back
So he strode to the camps of men
And lighted a blaze beyond their ken
But the spark was not his to part
Or so thought Zeus, the grand old fart
Alone poor Prometheus now growls
As an old crow eternally
Eats his bowels.
